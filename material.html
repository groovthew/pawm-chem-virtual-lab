Simple Present Tense

The simple present tense or present tense is one of the most basic tenses in English. We use present tense to talk about something that is currently going on, something that is habitually performed, or a state that generally or currently exists. Examples: Bob drives taxi.

Past Tense

The past tense is used to describe an activity or an event that has happened in the past or a past state of being and needs to include a time marker for when the event or action took place. Examples: Chris ate some pancakes yesterday.

Future Present Tense

The future tense is a verb tense used to describe an event or action that has not yet happened and is expected to happen in the future. Structural formula, Subject + shall/will+ verb (s/es) + object. Examples: He will be here soon.

Past Perfect Tense

The past perfect tense is used to describe an event that occurred before a completed action in the past. Formula is Subject + had + verb (ed) + object. Examples: He had gone when she became ill.

Future Continuous Tense

The future continuous tense is used to describe an ongoing action that will occur or occur in the future. Examples: He will be coming to visit us next week.
